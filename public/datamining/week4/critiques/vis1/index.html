Visualization Critique: NYT “Tracking Covid at U.S. Colleges and Universities”

Message

The New York Times’ university COVID-19 tracking visualization is an online compilation of nationwide university coronavirus cases, conveniently organized into an accordion menu of states. Upon expanding a state, individual universities are shown alongside case counts, locations (cities), and sets of colored bars representing each week from March 1 to September 2. The bars are colored in an intensity pattern, with light yellow representing fewer cases in the university’s county and dark red representing more cases. This all gives a general idea of how each university might be faring in the struggle to keep the coronavirus at bay among a population known to spread illness efficiently.

Critique

While the visualization is great at organizing its data to quickly find information about a specific university or set of universities in a state, it does have its downsides. Case counts may be precise here, but the colored weekly bars provide much less detail with their scale of “Fewer” to “More” cases. The bars represent how the surrounding county is faring with the coronavirus, which may be misleading when compared with how the more isolated university populations are doing. Overall, the visualization is great for at-a-glance gleaning of information, but lacks some depth needed to find deeper meaning in the numbers.
