Visualization Critique: NYT “Tracking the Real Coronavirus Death Toll in the United States”

Message

This data visualization informs the reader of the number of deaths in the United States above the expected value throughout 2020. This data is interpreted in the surrounding article as a potentially more accurate death toll relating to coronavirus. These excess deaths may be directly or indirectly related to COVID-19. For an example of indirect losses, deaths above normal may correspond to a lack of hospital beds for patients with complications from unrelated issues. It should be noted these excess deaths include the official coronavirus death numbers of around 200,000, leaving about 50,000 deaths unaccounted for.

Critique

As a simple bar chart, this visualization is a great, easy-to-read way to convey how weekly death counts in the U.S. are differing from the expected value (the x-axis origin). The chart is good for general estimates, but struggles to provide detail. The United States is large and varied, which is where the Times’ other state-specific visualizations come into play. The chart notes “recent weeks are likely undercounts,” likely in response to the change in nationwide coronavirus data reporting in the United States from the C.D.C. to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.
